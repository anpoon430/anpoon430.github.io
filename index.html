About


I am a Civil Engineering Graduate passionate about coding. Through learning Python and JavaScript I was able to apply problem solving skills from my engineering education as well as develop creativity skills by finding different approaches to problems. I am interested in the uses of software applications in older industries as ways to create value.

I graduated with a Bachelor's in Civil Engineering from the University of Toronto. In the previous summers I worked as a summer camp counselor and volunteered as a tutor.

The most important thing I learned from studying engineering was that engineering design is never isolated as a purely technical problem to be solved. Good designs always approach a problem 'from outside in' and considers how well they fit into our present world by beginning with how we encounter them in our daily lives.


What I'm doing now


I am currently self studying Python through Udacity's Data Analyst nanodegree and web development through Hack Reactor's Structure Study Program in order to transition into a software career. I am using Trello to track my progress and Toggl to track where my time is spent. When I am not studying, I enjoy reading books from a wide range of genres(The Bible, Christian Theology/History, Science Fiction, Business), strength training and working on personal projects.
